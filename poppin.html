Poppin

My take on the Sample App from Michael Hartl's Ruby on Rails Tutorial

Poppin was a Ruby on Rails app I made while completing the Coder Manual Complete Web Developer Bootcamp as a high school senior.

A description of the development and functionality of the sample app, as taken from the Rails Tutorial:

"You’ll develop the sample app using a combination of mockups, test-driven development (TDD), and integration tests. You’ll get started in Chapter 3 by creating static pages and then add a little dynamic content. You’ll take a quick detour in Chapter 4 to learn a little about the Ruby language underlying Rails. Then, in Chapter 5 through Chapter 12, you’ll complete the foundation for the sample application by making a site layout, a user data model, and a full registration and authentication system (including account activation and password resets). Finally, in Chapter 13 and Chapter 14 you’ll add microblogging and social features to make a working example site."

I showed it to some of my friends, and we started using it to message each other every day. Having an active user base provided me with plenty of bug reports and feature requests, and I kind of got carried away with adding popcorn puns and new features outside the scope of the course, including direct messaging, the ability to “like” messages, and the ability to see which users were currently online or recently active.
